Cooking Tips

Tip1: Clean as you cook to keep your workspace organized and manageable.
  1. Wipe down countertops and cutting boards immediately after use.
  2. Rinse utensils and cooking equipment as soon as you're done with them.
  3. Keep a trash bowl or bin nearby to collect scraps and waste as you go.

  • Store spices in a cool, dark place to maintain their flavor.

Cooking Glossary

Knife
A tool for slicing, cutting, etc., made from a metal head with a wooden, metal or remnant handle removed; also used as a weapon
Plate
A round vessel, usually more (shallow plates) or more (deep plates) recessed, used for serving food. Plates are made of various materials, most often ceramics (porcelain, faience), metal, glass, and nowadays also plastic.
Oven
A kitchen device used for baking or browning food. Currently, mini ovens are also produced, which have limited functionality, but are suitable for small kitchens in the form of built-in ovens or as portable ovens.
